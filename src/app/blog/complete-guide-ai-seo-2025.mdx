        <h3>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>
        <p>Many websites inadvertently block AI systems or create content that AI platforms can't effectively use. Understanding these common mistakes helps you avoid reducing your AI visibility:</p>

        <h4>Technical Blocking Issues</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Blocking AI crawlers in robots.txt</strong> - Accidentally preventing AI access to your content - Many sites use broad blocking rules that inadvertently include AI crawlers. Review your robots.txt file specifically for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot to ensure they have appropriate access.</li>
          <li><strong>Aggressive rate limiting</strong> that blocks legitimate AI crawlers - While protecting against malicious bots is important, overly strict rate limits can prevent AI systems from properly crawling your content. Configure rate limiting to allow reasonable access from known AI crawlers.</li>
          <li><strong>Requiring JavaScript</strong> for essential content - AI crawlers may not execute JavaScript, so content that's only available through JavaScript rendering might be invisible to AI systems. Ensure critical content is available in the initial HTML response.</li>
          <li><strong>Using meta robots "noindex"</strong> unnecessarily - This directive prevents AI systems from indexing your content. Only use noindex for content you genuinely don't want AI systems to access, not as a blanket directive for internal pages.</li>
        </ul>

        <h4>Content Structure Problems</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Burying key information</strong> in lengthy introductions - AI systems prefer content that gets to the point quickly. Avoid long preambles before providing the information users are seeking. Lead with answers, then provide supporting context.</li>
          <li><strong>Using vague headings</strong> that don't describe content - Headings like "Getting Started" or "Next Steps" don't help AI systems understand what information follows. Use descriptive headings that clearly indicate the content's topic and purpose.</li>
          <li><strong>Creating content without clear structure</strong> - Wall-of-text content is difficult for AI systems to parse and extract information from. Use headings, lists, and clear paragraph breaks to create scannable, extractable content.</li>
          <li><strong>Focusing on keywords over user intent</strong> - AI systems prioritize content that genuinely answers user questions over content optimized for specific keywords. Focus on providing comprehensive, helpful information rather than keyword density.</li>
        </ul>

        <h4>Attribution and Authority Issues</h4>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Lacking proper source attribution</strong> - AI systems value content that properly cites sources and provides verification paths. Always link to original sources and provide clear attribution for statistics and claims.</li>
          <li><strong>Publishing outdated information</strong> without updates - AI systems prefer current information, especially for rapidly changing topics. Regularly review and update your content to maintain accuracy and relevance.</li>
          <li><strong>Missing author and publication information</strong> - AI systems consider content authority when determining citation worthiness. Include clear author bylines, publication dates, and organizational information to establish credibility.</li>
          <li><strong>Ignoring E-E-A-T principles</strong> - Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness matter to AI systems just as they do to search engines. Demonstrate your qualifications and expertise in your content and author profiles.</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>Q: What's the most common AI SEO mistake?</h3>
        <p><strong>A:</strong> Blocking AI crawlers in robots.txt is the most critical mistake. Many websites inadvertently block GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or PerplexityBot, making their content invisible to these platforms regardless of other optimization efforts.</p>
